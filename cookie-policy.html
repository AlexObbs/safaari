2. WHAT ARE COOKIES?

2.1 Definition of Cookies and Similar Technologies

Cookies are small text files that are stored on your device (computer, mobile phone, tablet, etc.) by your web browser when you visit a website. They are widely used to make websites work more efficiently, as well as to provide information to the website owners. In addition to cookies, similar tracking technologies such as web beacons, pixel tags, and local storage may also be used.

2.2 How Cookies Work

When you visit our Website, your browser may store one or more cookies on your device. These cookies help the Website remember your actions and preferences over a period of time, so you don't have to re-enter your details every time you return. Cookies also enable us to collect aggregated data about website traffic and usage, which is crucial for improving the Website's functionality and user experience.

3. TYPES OF COOKIES USED ON OUR WEBSITE

3.1 Necessary/Essential Cookies

These cookies are essential for the operation of the Website. They enable basic functions such as page navigation, secure access to user accounts, and the proper functioning of our online booking services. Without these cookies, the Website cannot function properly.

3.2 Analytical Cookies (e.g., Google Analytics)

Analytical cookies allow us to collect data about how visitors use the Website. This includes tracking metrics such as page views, time spent on pages, and conversion rates. We use this data primarily to improve the Website's performance, understand user behavior, and optimize our content. Google Analytics is one such tool that helps us track and analyze traffic and user engagement.

3.3 Advertising and Marketing Cookies (e.g., Google Adsense)

Advertising cookies are used to deliver relevant advertisements to you based on your interests. These cookies also help measure the effectiveness of advertising campaigns and limit the number of times you see an ad. Google Adsense is one of the advertising services that may use cookies to serve personalized ads on our Website.

3.4 Functional Cookies

Functional cookies enhance your experience by remembering choices you make (such as language preferences or region settings) and providing more personalized features. These cookies do not collect any personally identifiable information.

4. PURPOSES OF USING COOKIES

4.1 Enhancing User Experience

Cookies allow the Website to remember your preferences and settings, making your subsequent visits more convenient and personalized. For instance, cookies help keep you logged in to your account and store your language preferences.

4.2 Website Performance and Analytics

The data collected via analytical cookies helps us understand how users interact with the Website. This information is used to identify areas for improvement, monitor website performance, and ensure that our content meets user needs.

4.3 Advertising and Monetization

Cookies help us deliver targeted advertising that is more relevant to your interests. By tracking your browsing habits and interactions on the Website, we can provide ads that are tailored to your preferences while also helping us generate revenue to maintain and improve our services.

5. DETAILED INFORMATION ON SPECIFIC COOKIES

5.1 Cookies Set by Our Website

We set several cookies on our Website to ensure smooth operation and a personalized experience. These include:

  • Session Cookies: Temporary cookies that expire once you close your browser. They are used to maintain session state as you navigate the Website.
  • Persistent Cookies: Cookies that remain on your device for a set period or until you delete them. They are used to remember your preferences and login details across visits.

5.2 Third-Party Cookies and Their Purposes

In addition to our own cookies, our Website uses cookies provided by third-party service providers, including:

  • Google Analytics Cookies: Used to track user interactions and gather statistical data about Website usage.
  • Google Adsense Cookies: Used to serve targeted advertisements based on your browsing history.
  • Other Marketing Cookies: These may be set by external advertising networks to measure the success of advertising campaigns and to provide personalized ad experiences.

7. HOW TO CONTROL AND MANAGE COOKIES

7.1 Browser Settings and Cookie Preferences

Most web browsers allow you to control cookies through their settings preferences. You can choose to block all cookies, allow only certain types of cookies, or delete cookies after each session. Instructions for managing cookies are usually available in your browser's "Help" section. Please note that disabling certain cookies may impact your ability to use some features of the Website.

7.2 Opt-Out Instructions for Google Analytics and Google Adsense

  • Google Analytics: To opt out of Google Analytics tracking, you can download and install the Google Analytics Opt-out Browser Add-on, available for most browsers. For more details, please visit Google's Opt-out Page.
  • Google Adsense: Google provides an option for users to opt out of personalized advertising. Visit Google's Ads Settings to manage your ad preferences and opt out of interest-based ads.

7.3 Using Third-Party Tools for Cookie Management

There are also third-party tools and browser extensions available that can help you manage and block cookies across multiple websites. We encourage you to explore these options if you wish to further customize your cookie settings.
